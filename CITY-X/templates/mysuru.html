Welcome to Mysuru

Mysuru, formerly known as Mysore, is a historic city in the southern state of Karnataka, India. Renowned for its rich cultural heritage and grandeur, Mysuru is often referred to as the "Cultural Capital of Karnataka." The city is famed for its majestic Mysore Palace, a prime example of Indo-Saracenic architecture and the residence of the Wodeyar dynasty that ruled the region for several centuries. Mysuru is also celebrated for the annual Dasara festival, a ten-day event marked by vibrant processions, cultural performances, and the iconic illumination of the Mysore Palace. The festival attracts visitors from across the globe, adding to the city's cultural vibrancy. In addition to its historical and cultural significance, Mysuru is known for its educational institutions, making it an important educational hub in the region. The city is also famous for its traditional crafts, including Mysore silk sarees and sandalwood carvings. Nestled at the base of the Chamundi Hills, Mysuru offers picturesque landscapes and a pleasant climate, making it a popular tourist destination. Notable attractions include the Chamundeshwari Temple, Brindavan Gardens, and the Mysuru Zoo. Overall, Mysuru's blend of historical grandeur, cultural richness, and natural beauty makes it a captivating destination with a distinct charm.

Chamundeshwari Temple

A Spiritual and Cultural Landmark Chamundeshwari Temple, perched atop the Chamundi Hills, is one of the most revered and ancient temples in Karnataka. It holds immense spiritual significance and offers visitors breathtaking views of Mysore city. Dedicated to Goddess Chamundeshwari, an incarnation of Goddess Durga, this temple is a must-visit for those interested in history, architecture, and spirituality. Mysore Palace:

₹1500.00

A Jewel of India's Royal Heritage Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is an architectural masterpiece and a significant cultural landmark situated in the heart of Mysore, Karnataka. It stands as a symbol of the grandeur and elegance of the Wodeyar dynasty, which ruled the Kingdom of Mysore for several centuries. The palace is not just a royal residence but a testament to the artistic and architectural ingenuity of the period. Its rich history, stunning architecture, and cultural significance make it a must-visit destination for tourists from around the world.

Mysore Zoo

₹1800.00

A Paradise for Wildlife Enthusiasts Mysore Zoo, officially known as the Sri Chamarajendra Zoological Gardens, is one of the oldest and most well-maintained zoos in India. Established in 1892 by Maharaja Chamaraja Wodeyar, the zoo is situated near the Mysore Palace and spans over 157 acres.
